### History and purpose behind schedule consolidation

The General Services Administration (GSA) has been working to consolidate its Multiple Award Schedules (MAS) program since 2016. The goal is to simplify the procurement process, reduce administrative burdens, and make it easier for federal agencies to find and award contracts.

Prior to consolidation, GSA had over 200 separate schedules, each with its own unique requirements and offerings. This complexity created confusion among contractors and federal agencies alike. To address this issue, GSA introduced a new, streamlined approach to contracting through the MAS program.

Under the consolidated schedule, all products and services are grouped into 10 SINs (Special Item Numbers), which correspond to broad categories such as Information Technology or Professional Services. Contractors can now offer their products and services across multiple schedules using a single contract, reducing administrative burdens and increasing flexibility.

For example, a contractor with expertise in IT services could previously have held contracts under multiple separate schedules, each requiring its own separate contract administration and reporting requirements. Under the consolidated schedule, they would only need to maintain one contract, but could still offer their services across all relevant SINs.

Contractors should note that while consolidation has reduced administrative complexities, it also requires a more strategic approach to marketing and selling under the new framework. To succeed in this environment, contractors will need to:

* Carefully assess which SINs align with their products and services
* Update their marketing materials and sales strategies accordingly
* Ensure compliance with all relevant GSA requirements

By understanding the history and purpose behind schedule consolidation, contractors can position themselves for success in the new contracting landscape.
